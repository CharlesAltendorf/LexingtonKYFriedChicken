About

Eating a double down from KFC.

Before starting college at the University of Kentucky, I didn't realize just how wild Lexington was for fried chicken. However, one simply cannot ignore the staggering popularity of fried chicken restaurants in the city. In putting together this study, I identified 31 locations representing 11 national chains as well as 3 local places. Lexington's population is about 330,000 people, meaning there is roughly 1 fried chicken joint for every 1,000 people. Click on the tiles above to find out more. Also feel free to contact me if you feel like I missed a spot.

Total Spots

Check out the interactive map

For these final 34 fried chicken spots I ended up choosing for this project, I attempted to limit to places I considered thier primary entree to be fried chicken fingers or sandwhiches. This excluded places that had other primary menu items like wings and burgers. Feel free to reach out if you have a good argument for other places to be added. Regardless, this map clearly shows there is a fried chicken joint for at least every 100 Lexington residents.

Local Spots

Check out the interactive map

In my opinion there are 3 local spots known for being predominently fried chicken oriented. I open to being told about ones I haven't heard of yet. When I was at UK, it was a ritual to take transplants to Indi's. I have yet to try Grime's, but it must be doing ok now that it has two locations. Oddly, Grimes doesn't have a website I could find.

Chick-fil-a Locations

Check out the interactive map

With 9 locations in the Lexington city limits alone, Chick-fil-a is by far the most dominant chicken chain in the area. Originally from humble cafe roots in 1950s Atlanta, Chick-fil-a began expanding into malls throughout the 1970s. In the 1990s, Chick-fil-a began franchising out freestanding restaurants and has been rapidly expanding since. It is hard to envision it's dominance in Lexington slowing anytime soon. I used to work at the Turfland location during my undergrad.

KFC Locations

Check out the interactive map

No name is more well known world-wide for chicken than Kentucky Fried Chicken. KFC has had some ups and downs since the 1990s, but still has a strong presence in Lexington. There are 5 locations in the Lexington city limits. With it's continued attempts to innovate it's menu, I anticipate KFC to also be around for a long time even if it's less dominant.

Zaxby's Locations

Check out the interactive map

In creating these maps, I was quite astonished to see Zaxby's has 5 Lexington locations like KFC. Zaxby's is a much newer chain than KFC or Chick-fil-a, getting it's start in Athens, Georgia in 1990. I have tried it a couple times but still don't go out of my way for it. Feel free to right into me if you're a big fan and tell my why.

Raising Cane's Locations

Check out the interactive map

Another up and coming chicken chain, Raising Cane's started in Baton Rouge, LA in 1996. The first Lexington location opened during my undergrad at UK, and now there are 4 in the city limits. I think the low overhead, simple menu and quick service will keep Cane's around awhile.

Stagnant Spots

Check out the interactive map

Started by Colonel's Saunders cousin in 1966, Lee's Famous Recipe has been in Kentucky for a long time. At one point, Lee's even had 3 locations in Lexington. Popeye's was started in greater New Orleans in the early 70s and has also been in Lexington a long time. Both chains seem to just be hanging around Lexington without much desire for expansion.

Insurgent Spots

Check out the interactive map

Several new fried chicken spots continue to open in Lexington. Gus's came from Memphis and chose a spot in the reconstructed intersection at Main, Vine and Midland. Joella's came from Louisville and opened near chevy chase and has put in a second location in hamburg. Richie's has come in from Cincinnati and has chosen an intersting spot on New Circle Road. Dave's came from Los Angeles and took over a defunct QDoba location on Richmond Road. TKK is chicken chain that's come to Lexington all the way from Taiwan! It will be very interesting to see which of these chains become the next big thing.
